\section{Literature study}

Ground-effect vehicles have been seriously studied and developed since the early 50's. Their applications, although initially focussed on military transportation, have since expanded to include commercial cargo and personell transport. Even with this seemingly rich history of research, uncertainties remain regarding the aerodynamic behaviour of these vehicles in ground effect, especially when considering modern design features and materials. This literature study aims to review the existing body of knowledge on ground-effect vehicles, focusing on aerodynamic characteristics, stability, and design considerations.

\subsection{Ground Effect Aerodynamics}

Purpose: establish what ground effect does to aerodynamics.\\
Include:\\
\begin{itemize}
    \item Lift enhancement mechanisms near ground
    \item Pressure distribution changes
    \item Induced drag reduction
    \item Sensitivity to height-to-chord ratio (h/c)
\end{itemize}

Focus on:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Quantitative trends (e.g. lift coefficient vs h/c)
    \item Differences from free-flight aerodynamics
\end{itemize}
This justifies why airfoil choice matters differently in ground effect.
