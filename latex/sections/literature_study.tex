\section{Literature study}

The history of Ground Effect Vehicles (GEVs) dates back to the early 20th century when engineers and inventors began exploring the potential of utilizing the aerodynamic ground effect phenomenon for transportation. The ground effect occurs when an aircraft flies close to a surface, such as water or land, resulting in increased lift and reduced drag due to the interaction between the wings and the surface.
